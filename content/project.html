CodeCats;

METRO NEKO




Overview



Topic


Our project proposal is to design a 2D-side scroller game so that it can be compatible on both iOS and Android mobile operating systems. We want to make the project simple and easy to understand yet challenging enough to keep the player engaged. The player must complete each level in the most time efficient way, while collecting coins and avoiding depleting health levels. Successful players receive higher bonus points on the scoreboard.


We imagine the game aesthetics to have a dark theme. Set in a dystopian futuristic city, backed by a neon skyline backdrop, our main character (a cat) will have to maneuver its way through the city – jumping from platform to platform, avoiding various obstacles and enemies along the way. The music will complement the overall gloomy vibe, with eerie techno music accompanying the players’ gameplay experience. We hope to complete at least Level 1, to serve as the basis for future iterations and to provide an expectation the skills required by the player to succeed as the game continues to the next levels. We hope the players will experience it as an homage to old-school gaming culture in a romanticised 2D world.



Motivation


Our project is focused on meeting current market demands. Statistically, 81% of gamers play on mobile devices, with puzzle/platform games the second-most popular genre after simulation games (GWI 2021). Side-scrolling games have been popular since the eighties and remain so: of the top 100 ranked games on the Apple App Store, 50 are 2-D titles. Side-scrolling platformers are a style of game everyone can enjoy, regardless of age or ability, and thus have remained popular despite advances in technology. The top reasons for playing games include for enjoyment, storylines and escaping from reality (GWI 2021); as such, with its interesting narrative and simple controls, our game will appeal to a broad range of gamers. By completing this project, we would be able to demonstrate to future employers our experience in all stages of developing a commercially viable product, including conceptualisation, market research, planning, programming and editing.



Landscape


While many side-scrolling platformers already exist, the huge demand for mobile games means enthusiasts are constantly seeking fresh new experiences. Among the most popular titles are Super Mario Run, Sonic the Hedgehog 2 and Rayman Jungle Run. What separates our product from these types of games is a more mature aesthetic and narrative, which will attract adult gamers; having said this, with its simple controls and appealing main character, our game will still be accessible for all types of gamers. The main frustrations consumers have reported regarding their gaming experiences are games that do not run smoothly, are too expensive to progress in, or too hard to play. Our product promises to provide consistently high framerates, will not require in-app purchases, and will be challenging enough to engage players without the need to master a difficult set of controls.

Aims


In this project, we aim to develop the first level of our game as a proof-of-concept. The game will support a widescreen 16:9 ratio, and the characters and certain objects will be animated. It will also include sound effects corresponding to the playable character and enemies’ actions, and theme music. In terms of programming, the C# script will implement simple 2D physics to simulate gravity and allow the user to control the playable character. A list of specific goals for each of these categories follows:



Static Visuals and Animations


To engage players, our game needs to include an aesthetically pleasing environment, and interesting animations. We will create original artwork for the background, which will scroll at a different aspect to the character and other objects to simulate depth. Animations will be developed for the main character’s motions, including running, jumping, falling and dying, as well as for the movement of enemies and the rotation of coins.



Sound Effects


To create atmosphere and act as feedback to players, our game should have sound effects that correspond to the action of the scene. Original theme music will be composed, of approximately one minute in length; this will loop as the user plays. Short tunes will also be required to signal a player’s achievements at the completion of the level. We will create individual sounds for the enemies, and for the playable character for instances of falling off a platform, touching an enemy, collecting coins, and completing the game. All sounds will be in stereo.



Programming


The main character must respond the player’s inputs, and the game itself needs to possess realistic physics to support interaction between the main character and the environment.  The main character must be able to move left and right, and jump. Enemies should begin moving left and right once they are visible. The main character should fall if not standing on a platform, while the coins need to remain suspended in the air until collected. At the end of the level, the player’s time and score will be written to a locally stored XML file.



Plans & Progress


What are we building?


Code Cats are developing a single player, 2D action platformer game titled Metro Neko. With its side-scroller dynamic, Metro Neko will be easy to understand whilst its increasingly challenging gameplay, unique characters and vibrant atmosphere will keep players engaged.

Who is our target audience?


According to WePC, there are 2.7 billion gamers globally (WEPC 2021). Of this, Asia-Pacific accounts for 49.2%, North America at 25.1%, Europe at 18.6%, Latin America at 3.8% and the Middle East & Africa at 3.4% (WEPC 2021). Taking this into account, along with the average age of gamers being 33 (GWI 2021), Metro Neko’s target audience will be consumers aged 18-34, marketed towards those in Asia-Pacific and North America.


Competition:


Metro Neko’s competition is mostly mobile games. Google Play Store and Apple App store are the main targets of the game. We mainly focus on user interface, and we are making the game more user friendly while keeping the promotions and ads to help with finding the right audience. We are also looking to use platforms that boost ranking of Metro Neko in Google Play Store ranking. Lastly, we will be creating a website to accompany the game to introduce it to more people across the web.

With multi-device ownership not being uncommon in today’s age, 81% of consumers play games on their smartphones and tablets whilst 65% play games on their PC/laptops (GWI 2021). In order for Metro Neko to reach as many players as possible, it will be made available across multiple platforms, first being introduced on mobile devices (Android and iOS) with further development necessary before being made available on PC.

Interactive Design Features:


The gameplay consists of multiple basic interactive design elements. This includes:


Words: Providing users with gameplay tutorial information that is clear and easy to understand, also allowing them to re-read and access the information from the main menu in case they have dismissed them by accident.


Audio visuals: Users are also allowed to interact with the game using videos, soundtracks, images, typography, the user interface and of course the gameplay.


Time: The game consists of levels and a progression system. Each level gets harder and more challenging which takes longer to progress and more rewarding as the player progresses through each level.


User interface interactions:


Trailer: The user is presented with a short 10-15 second video outlining the story and introducing the main character as well as the protagonist.


Menu: The user is then presented with the main menu of the game, this includes the logo and the name of the game, the soundtrack of the game as well as the start menu. The menu consists of “Play”, “Tutorials” and “Exit”.


Story: Upon starting the game for the first time, there is a longer video expanding on the story, using audio visuals and words at the same time.


Gameplay: The game starts presenting the main character and the first level to overcome, the tutorials are interactive in a sense that they will run as necessary in different situations. They will pause the game and let the user read and dismiss by clicking/tapping on the display. The second playthrough will not have the tutorials enabled. Health bar and score system also available to the users as visuals.


User Interface controls:


The UI controls are as follows:


Menu:


  1. Touch Screen: tap on the menu items to perform the task described.
  2. Non-touch screen: left click using a mouse or moving up and down with directional pads on the controller and then selecting using the A/X button.

To move left and right:


  1. Touch screen: Hold the analogue displayed on the screen and move it to the right to move the character to the right and vice versa.
  2. Non-touch screen: Use keyboard arrow keys or controllers directional pad to move left and right respectively.

To jump:


  1. Touch screen: Tap anywhere on the right side of the screen to perform jump action.
  2. Non-touch screen: Use keyboard space bar or controllers A/X button to perform jump action.

Exit/access main menu:


  1. Touch screen: Tap on the gear icon displayed on the top right corner of the display, then tap the selection then proceed.
  2. Non-touch screen: use the mouse to click on the gear icon displayed on the top right corner of the display and then click the selection and proceed.

Gameplay


Object of the game:


The object of the game is for the player to get to the last level, putting a stop to the evil Miyako, locking her up for good once and for all. To achieve this, players must progress through 10 levels, with the difficulty increasing as each level progresses. Across each level, players will need to avoid/destroy enemies whilst collecting coins, which earn them points towards their overall high score.


Gameplay description:


Game play is consisting of jumping, running, health bar and score system:


Jumping: it allows the player to jump on the platforms, jump over obstacles to avoid enemies and dangers, and to jump on the enemies to kill them. Failing to jump on platforms or hitting obstacles or enemies will result in death and game over.


Running: it allows the players to move around the level as well as choosing different paths and platforms.


Health bar: A visual representation of the main characters health. Upon being hit by an enemy the health bar decreases.


Score system: A visual representation of the score achieved by the players.


Game play specifications:


Genre: The genre is an action platformer, and the gameplay is around jumping, running, and overcoming obstacle and enemies along the way to finish each level.


Difficulty: The difficulty of the game increases every time a player fails to complete a level, as a result increasing the number of obstacles and enemies. However, completing a level successfully will increase the difficulty of the next level.


Time to complete: The game should take less than 1 hour to complete all 10 levels. To get both endings you would require 2 hours to complete the game.


Level description:


Each level is consisting of a different environment, platforms, obstacles, and enemies:


Environment: There will be 10 levels and each level represent a different time of the year and season.


Platforms: Platforms will be placed differently across each level, and they will follow the same environmental look and feel.


Obstacles: Obstacles will be placed on the platforms and sometimes under the platforms to add more dangers to each level.


Enemies: Enemies will roam on the platforms and guarding in tricky to jump situations.


Sound design description:


Opening cinematic soundtrack: To include Presto tempo music using 168-200 beats per minute. This is to increase the tension of the fight shown in the cinematic.


Game story soundtrack: To include Larghissimo tempo music using under 24 beats per minute to add more immersive dynamics to the game story.


Main Menu soundtrack: Same music from the Game story soundtrack to continue playing in the main menu.


Level soundtrack: To include Moderato tempo music using 108-120 beats per minute. This is to increase the tension and immersions of players into the environment.


Senshi: To have natural serious human voice, with some characteristics from Cats like, purring and meowing.


Miyako: To have high pitch serious human voice, with some characteristics from Cats like, purring and meowing.


White ghost cats: To have low pitch serious human voice whispering, with some characteristics from Cats like, purring and meowing.


Zombie cats: To have zombie like moaning, with some characteristics from Cats like, purring and meowing.

Testing



Testing our development will be a repetitive process which each subsequent version being tested extensively for bugs and errors. Professionally, game testing follows a structure regardless of the size and scope of the game developed*. With each new version, test documents need to be revisited and edited to adjust specifications and new added features or possible weaknesses. Any new bugs and errors should be subsequently identified. The first step would be to classify the user and system requirements – functional, non- functional testing. As the current scope of the project is somewhat limited these categories are not yet extensive. Functional testing involves testing items that are related to the user’s requirements and can be manually tested. Non-functional testing conversely refers to items that are related on the user’s expectations and are difficult to perform manually.


  • Functional: User-Interface, gameplay, and functionality.
  • Non-functional: Security, efficiency, survivability, usability.

Based on these classifications, first, the functional aspects of the game will be tested. This segment of our testing methodology involves extensively testing for feature and bug issues. Manually testing the interface and gameplay as each subsequent change and adjustment is made. This phase will extend through the main development of the game. Examples of this testing phase are manually checking colors, background, audio, animations, user interfaces, resolution, and gameplay. Unity, the tool we are using to develop Metro Neko offers an extensive debugging tool – MonoDevelop IDE which we will utilize to debug the programming side of our game. As Unity also offers conditional breakpoints at lines, this is another technical aspect of our main development tool that will greatly aid in testing.


Once a bare-bones mockup is ready with core functionality and all functional aspects are accounted for, non-functional testing can begin. This segment of our testing strategy will deal with aspects that are harder to test manually such as efficiency and user experience, this will require additional users testing our development. This stage might include an open alpha or beta test where we would offer the game to be tested by the public. A small sample size of users at this stage would be sufficient i.e., <=100. These users will be found in a few different places – they can be sourced personally (friends and family), Reddit posts on beta test forums, and an email list on our marketing media.


Success in the testing segment of our development will be achieved when no major bugs or vulnerabilities are left on the game, the gameplay is smooth and responsive, and we have a satisfactory user experience reported from our beta tests. As testing is a repetitive and ongoing process as the game is further developed and new features are added testing will continue and these goals will be extended.

Risks



Risk Assessment


Risks identified below are a projection of both immediate project management risks and hypothesised risks for the ensuing game launch.





The risk assessment impact keys are as follows:


  • 1 = Negligible
  • 2 = Minor
  • 3 = Moderate
  • 4 = Significant
  • 5 = Severe

Identified risks in order of probability:


  • Major Risks (High)

    • Marketing

      • Strategy [5]: Poor timing of release, poor marketing, poor follow-up of deliverables.
      • Competitors [5]: Game launch will be impacted by the influx of competitors delivering similar games at the same time on iOS and android.
      • Marketability [4]: Trailer release and game launch may be subject to moderate demand within a highly competitive environment for 2d side scroller games.


  • Moderate Risks (Medium)

    • UX design

      • Performance [5]: Bugs and performance issues may impact gameplay and user experience.
      • Aesthetics [3]: Overall design/interface of the game may not appeal to target market.
      • Feature creep [1]: Overloading features and functionality in the UX affecting speed and overall user experience.


  • Minor Risks (Low)

    • Development

      • Timeframe [3]: Feasibility of project will be impacted by underestimating timeline and product release deadlines.
      • Skills [2]: 2 out of 6 of the team members have prior experience working with Unity; 2 out of 6 of the team members have prior programming experience; 4 out of 6 of the team members have prior experience in design (Adobe, Photoshop, Illustrator, other); 1 out of 6 of the team members have prior experience in audio editing.
      • Software [2]: Unity unable to synchronise or accept various design formats, incompatibility issues.


Risk management:



  • Critical High Priority Tasks

    • Testing:

      • Test for performance and vulnerabilities - incremental user testing needed prior to game launch.


  • Major Risks

    • Marketing:

      • Strategy [5]: Ensure market research is done prior to release and strategically time the release date for product launch. Marketing will need to consider the target market and effectively deliver the features expected for version 1.
      • Competitors [5]: Correctly assess niche competitors releasing 2D and 3D games via the App Store and Google Play.
      • Marketability [4]: We can mitigate this risk through careful analysis of research into 2d scroller games; niche market into retro arcades.


  • Moderate Risks

    • UX design:

      • Performance [5]: Test for performance and vulnerabilities - incremental user testing needed prior to game launch.
      • Aesthetics [3]: Design/interface of the game must match the expectations and experience of retro arcade side-scrollers yet stand out amongst such selections.
      • Feature creep [1]: Ensure feasibility of project for version 1 and keep it simple yet functional.


  • Minor Risks

    • Development:

      • Timeframe [3]: Plan out our project incrementally to ensure all aspects are covered prior to game launch/release of version 1. Ensure all team members work seamlessly throughout the project plan.
      • Skills [2]: Low risk due to the repertoire of various creative and technical skills of our team members.
      • Software [2]: Unity reviews indicate it is simple for beginner programmers. There are substantial tutorials and user support documentation available online if support is needed.


Group processes and communications


Communications are a key facet of our workflow as a team. We will have two set meetings a week for the duration of our development. The service Microsoft teams is invaluable for this process. In the meetings we go through our work so far and resolve any issues. We also use the service Discord as a more informal but regular form of communication. Any minor issues that arise can be discussed and resolved to some degree more expeditiously using text communication in Discord. The file service in Microsoft Teams serves as a de-facto version collaboration tool, similar to GitHub for text files and documents where we communicate via the Code Cats discord server or direct messages in discord and make changes and edits collaboratively to the documents in Teams.


In the event that a group member is unable to attend meetings or communicate regularly, we will take several steps to attempt to resolve the issue. It is always possible and understandable that issues beyond the control of individual members arise, and as a team it is important to come to a compromise that is emphatic and realistic but also productive. In case of a situation like this, the first step would be to have a discussion with said member about the issue.


Once the issue is determined and discussed appropriately, steps can be taken to resolve it - for example reassigning said member to work that is not an immediate priority. Alternatively, current work deadlines can be adjusted or extended. If the only issue is communication, the team could require more consistent communication or something more personal like direct messages to make sure work is being done in a timely manner. As long as the member contributes equally there are many compromises that can be made.
